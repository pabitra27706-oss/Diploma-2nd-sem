Simple Harmonic Motion (SHM)

Definition

Simple Harmonic Motion is a periodic motion where the restoring force is directly proportional to the displacement and acts in the direction opposite to displacement.

F ∝ -x   or   a ∝ -x

Expressions in SHM

Displacement: y = A sin(ωt + φ) or y = A cos(ωt + φ)

Velocity: v = Aω cos(ωt + φ) = ω√(A² - y²)

Acceleration: a = -ω²y

Time period: T = 2π/ω

Frequency: f = 1/T = ω/(2π)

Vibrations: Cantilever · Free · Damped · Forced

Cantilever vibration: time period T = 2π√(m/k) or for beam, T = 2π√(δ/g) where δ = static deflection.

Free Vibrations

System oscillates at natural frequency without external force after initial disturbance. No energy loss (ideal).

Damped Vibrations

Amplitude decreases due to energy loss (friction, air resistance). Examples: pendulum in air, shock absorbers.

Forced Vibrations

System driven by external periodic force. Vibrates at driving frequency. Resonance occurs when driving frequency = natural frequency.

Cantilever Time Period

A cantilever of length 1m, mass 0.5kg at free end, flexural rigidity EI = 100 Nm². Find time period. (δ = mgL³/(3EI))

δ = (0.5×9.81 × 1³)/(3×100) = 4.905/300 = 0.01635 m
T = 2π√(δ/g) = 2π√(0.01635/9.81) = 2π√0.001667 = 2π×0.0408 = 0.256 s

Wave Motion · Transverse · Longitudinal · Progressive Wave

〰️

Transverse

Particles vibrate perpendicular to wave direction. Example: light waves, waves on string.

🔊

Longitudinal

Particles vibrate parallel to wave direction. Example: sound waves.

Wave Parameters
  • Wave velocity (v): speed at which wave travels
  • Frequency (f): number of oscillations per second (Hz)
  • Wavelength (λ): distance between two consecutive crests/troughs
  • Relation: v = f λ
Plane Progressive Wave Equation

y = A sin(ωt - kx)   or   y = A sin(2πft - 2πx/λ)

where A = amplitude, ω = 2πf, k = 2π/λ (wave number).

Principle of Superposition & Beats

When two waves of slightly different frequencies superimpose, they produce beats – periodic variation in intensity.

Beat frequency = |f₁ - f₂|

Beat Frequency

Two sound waves of frequencies 256 Hz and 260 Hz. Find beat frequency.

f_beat = |260 - 256| = 4 Hz

Acoustics of Buildings

Reverberation

Reverberation is the persistence of sound in an enclosed space due to multiple reflections after the source stops.

Reverberation Time (RT)

Time taken for sound to decay by 60 dB (or to 10⁻⁶ of original intensity). Sabine's formula:

RT = 0.161 V / A

where V = volume (m³), A = total absorption (sabins).

Control of RT

  • Use absorbing materials (curtains, carpets, acoustic tiles)
  • Adjust room volume and shape
  • Place diffusers to scatter sound
Noise

Unwanted sound. Can be controlled by soundproofing, absorption, and isolation.

Coefficient of Absorption (α)

α = (Absorbed energy) / (Incident energy). For open window, α = 1 (perfect absorption).

Total absorption A = Σ αᵢ Sᵢ (sabins).

Reverberation Time Calculation

A hall of volume 500 m³ has total absorption 200 sabins. Find RT.

RT = 0.161 × 500 / 200 = 80.5 / 200 = 0.4025 s

Ultrasonic Waves: Properties & Applications

Introduction

Ultrasonic waves are sound waves with frequency > 20 kHz (above human hearing range).

Properties

  • High frequency, short wavelength
  • Highly directional (can be focused)
  • Can travel long distances in solids/liquids
  • Can produce cavitation in liquids
  • Reflect from small discontinuities

Engineering Applications

  • Non-destructive testing (NDT) – crack detection
  • Sonar (underwater navigation)
  • Ultrasonic cleaning
  • Welding plastics/metals
  • Flow measurement

Medical Applications

  • Ultrasound imaging (sonography)
  • Physiotherapy (deep heating)
  • Lithotripsy (breaking kidney stones)
  • Doppler ultrasound (blood flow)
