Basic Optical Laws: Reflection · Refraction · Refractive Index

Reflection

Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection. Incident ray, reflected ray, and normal lie in same plane.

Refraction (Snell's Law)

n₁ sinθ₁ = n₂ sinθ₂

where n = refractive index of medium.

Refractive Index

n = speed of light in vacuum / speed of light in medium = c/v

Also n₂₁ = n₂/n₁ (relative refractive index).

Lens Formula
1/f = 1/v - 1/u

where f = focal length, v = image distance, u = object distance (sign convention: u is negative for real object).

Lens Maker's Formula (no deduction)
1/f = (μ - 1)(1/R₁ - 1/R₂)

where μ = refractive index of lens material, R₁, R₂ = radii of curvature.

Power of Lens

P = 1/f (in meters). Unit: Diopter (D).

Convex: P positive; Concave: P negative.

Magnification (m)

m = height of image / height of object = v/u

For lens, m = f / (f+u) etc.

Numerical Problem 1

An object is placed 30cm from a convex lens of focal length 20cm. Find image distance and magnification.

1/f = 1/v - 1/u, with u = -30cm, f = +20cm
1/20 = 1/v - 1/(-30) ⇒ 1/20 = 1/v + 1/30
1/v = 1/20 - 1/30 = (3-2)/60 = 1/60 ⇒ v = 60 cm (real image)
m = v/u = 60/(-30) = -2 (inverted, magnified)
Numerical Problem 2

A concave lens has focal length -15cm. Object at 25cm. Find image position and magnification.

u = -25cm, f = -15cm
1/f = 1/v - 1/u ⇒ 1/(-15) = 1/v - 1/(-25)
-1/15 = 1/v + 1/25 ⇒ 1/v = -1/15 - 1/25 = -(5+3)/75 = -8/75
v = -9.375 cm (virtual image on same side)
m = v/u = (-9.375)/(-25) = +0.375 (erect, diminished)
Power of Lens

Two lenses of powers +2D and -1D are in contact. Find net power and focal length.

P = P₁ + P₂ = 2 + (-1) = +1 D
f = 1/P = 1/1 = 1 m = 100 cm (convex)

Total Internal Reflection · Critical Angle · Optical Fiber

Critical Angle (C)

sin C = n₂/n₁   (for n₁ > n₂, when angle in denser medium gives 90° in rarer).

Conditions for TIR
  • Light must travel from denser to rarer medium.
  • Angle of incidence > critical angle.
Applications in Optical Fiber

Optical fibers use TIR to guide light along core (high refractive index) surrounded by cladding (lower index).

Used in telecommunications, endoscopy, sensors.

Numerical Problem 3

Refractive index of glass is 1.5. Find critical angle for glass-air interface.

sin C = 1/μ = 1/1.5 = 0.6667
C = sin⁻¹(0.6667) = 41.8°

Optical Instruments: Microscope & Telescope

Simple Microscope

Single convex lens. Magnification when image at D (25cm):

m = 1 + D/f

When image at infinity: m = D/f

[Object within focal point → virtual image]

Compound Microscope

Two lenses: objective (f₀) and eyepiece (fₑ). Magnification:

m = m₀ × mₑ = (L/f₀) × (D/fₑ)

where L = tube length (distance between lenses).

Astronomical Telescope (Refracting)

Objective (large f₀) and eyepiece (small fₑ). Magnification for normal adjustment:

m = f₀ / fₑ

Image at infinity, inverted.

Simple Microscope

A simple microscope has focal length 5cm. Find magnification at D = 25cm (near point).

m = 1 + D/f = 1 + 25/5 = 1 + 5 = 6
Compound Microscope

Focal lengths: f₀ = 1cm, fₑ = 2cm, tube length L = 15cm. Find magnification.

m = (L/f₀) × (D/fₑ) = (15/1) × (25/2) = 15 × 12.5 = 187.5
Astronomical Telescope

f₀ = 100cm, fₑ = 5cm. Find magnification.

m = f₀/fₑ = 100/5 = 20

Wave Optics: Interference & Diffraction (Qualitative)

Interference of Light

Superposition of two coherent light waves produces alternate bright and dark fringes (constructive and destructive interference).

Condition for constructive: path difference = nλ

Destructive: path difference = (2n+1)λ/2

Example: Young's double slit experiment.

Diffraction of Light

Bending of light around obstacles or through apertures. Central maximum is brightest with secondary maxima/minima.

Single slit diffraction: width of central maximum = 2λD/d

Examples: Diffraction grating, CD/DVD reflection colors.

Comparison

Interference: from two separate sources; fringes equally spaced.

Diffraction: from single source/wavefront; central maximum wider, intensity falls off.
