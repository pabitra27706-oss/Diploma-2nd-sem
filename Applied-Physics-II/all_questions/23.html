APPLIED PHYSICS - II
2023
Time Allowed: 2.5 Hours
Full Marks: 60
Answer to Question No. 1 is compulsory and answer any Five (05) Questions from the rest.
1. Answer the following questions (any five): [5 × ? = 10 marks]
i. In which position the velocity of a particle executing SHM is maximum? Explain.
ii. On what factors does the focal length of a lens depend?
iii. Is the electric potential necessarily be zero at a point where the electric field strength is zero? Explain.
iv. What are the differences between Peltier effect and Joule effect?
v. A wire cuts across a flux of 0.002 Weber in 0.12 seconds. What is the emf induced in the wire?
vi. Write down the basic principle of operation of a solar photo voltaic cell.
vii. What are the unique properties of LASER?
viii. Mention three uses of nanotechnology.
[10 marks]
2.
(a) The equation of a particle vibrating in SHM is \(y = 5 \sin \frac{2\pi}{5}(10t + x)\), where \(y\) and \(x\) are in m and t is in s. Find out the frequency and amplitude of vibration.
(b) Write down a few differences between Oscillatory and Periodic motion.
(c) What is ultrasonic wave? Write down the properties and applications of it.
[2 + 3 + (1 + 3) = 9 marks]
3.
(a) A lens is composed of different layers made of two different materials. A point object is placed on the axis. How many images of the object will be formed and why?
(b) Calculate the speed of light in a medium whose critical angle is \(45^{\circ}\)
(c) Draw a ray diagram to show image formation when the convex lens produces a real, inverted and magnified image of the object.
(d) Write two features to distinguish between the interference patterns in Young's double slit experiment with the diffraction pattern obtained due to a single slit.
[3 + 2 + 2 + 2 = 9 marks]
4.
(a) Compare conductor, semiconductor and insulator in terms of energy band diagram.
(b) A student wants to use two p-n junction diodes to convert alternating current into direct current. Draw the labelled circuit diagram he/she would use and explain it.
(c) Draw the typical input and output characteristics of an n-p-n transistor in CE configuration. Show how these characteristics can be used to determine (i) the input resistance (r), and (ii) current amplification factor (β).
[3 + (2 + 1) + 3 = 9 marks]
5.
(a) State Lenz's law. Does it obey the principle of conservation of energy?
(b) Deduce an expression for the magnitude of the magnetic field at the centre of a circular coil of N turns.
(c) A straight wire carrying current of 12A is bent into a semi-circular arc of radius 0.02m. Find the magnitude of the magnetic field at the centre of the arc.
[(2+2) + 2 + 3 = 9 marks]
6.
(a) State Biot-Savart law and express this law in the vector form.
(b) An electron travelling with velocity \(30~\mathrm{cm s}^{-1}\) enters a region of uniform magnetic field \(0.2\mathrm{T}\) acting perpendicular to its paths. Calculate the magnitude of force on the electron. (charge of electron \(= 1.6\times 10^{-19}\mathrm{C}\))
(c) State and explain Lenz's law.
(d) A galvanometer of resistance \(100\Omega\) gives full scale deflection when a current of \(500\mu \mathrm{A}\) passes through it. How can this be converted to a voltmeter reading up to \(5\mathrm{V}\)? Draw necessary circuit diagram.
[(2+1) + 2 + 2 + 2 = 9 marks]
7.
(a) What are the differences between intrinsic and extrinsic semiconductors?
(b) Calculate the tube voltage required to be applied to an X-ray tube to get minimum wavelength 1Å. (Planck's constant \(\mathrm{h} = 6.626\times 10^{-34}\mathrm{J.s}\))
(c) Define population inversion. Discuss some method to achieve population inversion.
[3 + 2 + (1+3) = 9 marks]
8.
(a) Draw the curve showing the variation of intensity with wavelength of X-rays and mark cut off wavelength, continuous & characteristic X-rays. Write down two important applications of X-rays.
(b) Draw the energy level diagram of He-Ne Laser.
(c) State some advantages of optical fibre over conducting wire.
[(3+2) + 2 + 2 = 9 marks]
