Electrostatics Fundamentals

Coulomb's Law

Force between two point charges: F = k q₁q₂ / r²

where k = 1/(4πε₀) = 9×10⁹ N·m²/C², ε₀ = permittivity of free space.

Unit of charge: Coulomb (C).

Electric Field (E)

E = F/q₀ = kQ/r² (for point charge). Unit: N/C or V/m.

Electric Lines of Force

  • Start from +ve, end on -ve charges
  • Never intersect
  • Tangent gives direction of E
  • Closer lines → stronger field
  • Perpendicular to conductor surface

Electric Flux (Φ)

Φ = E·A = EA cosθ for uniform field. Unit: N·m²/C.

Electric Potential (V)

V = W/q₀ (work per unit charge). Potential difference: ΔV = V_B - V_A = -∫ E·dl

Unit: Volt (V) = J/C.

Gauss Law · Field due to Charged Sphere

Gauss Law (Statement)

The net electric flux through any closed surface is equal to 1/ε₀ times the net charge enclosed.

∮ E·dA = Q_enclosed / ε₀
Application: Field due to Charged Sphere

For a uniformly charged sphere of radius R, total charge Q:

  • Outside (r ≥ R): E = (1/(4πε₀)) Q/r² (like point charge)
  • Inside (r < R): E = (1/(4πε₀)) Q r / R³ (linearly increasing)
Numerical Problem 1

A sphere of radius 0.1m has charge 2μC. Find electric field at distance 0.3m from center.

Outside, E = kQ/r² = 9×10⁹ × 2×10⁻⁶ / (0.3)² = 9×10⁹ × 2×10⁻⁶ / 0.09
= 18000 / 0.09 = 2×10⁵ N/C

Capacitors: Working, Types, Capacitance

➕ ➖
Capacitor & Working

A capacitor stores electrical energy in an electric field between two conductors separated by insulator (dielectric).

Working: When connected to battery, charge accumulates on plates until potential difference equals battery voltage.

Types of Capacitors

  • Parallel plate capacitor
  • Cylindrical capacitor
  • Spherical capacitor
  • Electrolytic capacitor
  • Ceramic, Mica, Tantalum

Capacitance (C)

C = Q / V (charge per unit potential difference). Unit: Farad (F).

1 F = 1 C/V. Practical units: μF, nF, pF.

Parallel Plate Capacitor Formula
C = ε₀ A / d   (without dielectric)

where A = plate area, d = separation, ε₀ = 8.85×10⁻¹² F/m.

With dielectric: C = κ ε₀ A / d, where κ = dielectric constant.

Numerical Problem 2

Parallel plate capacitor has area 0.1 m², separation 1mm. Find capacitance.

C = ε₀ A/d = 8.85×10⁻¹² × 0.1 / 0.001 = 8.85×10⁻¹³ / 10⁻³ = 8.85×10⁻¹⁰ F = 885 pF

Series & Parallel Combinations

Series Combination

1/C_eq = 1/C₁ + 1/C₂ + ...

Charge same on each capacitor. Voltage divides.

Parallel Combination

C_eq = C₁ + C₂ + ...

Voltage same across each. Charge divides.

Numerical Problem 3 (Series)

Two capacitors 10μF and 20μF are connected in series. Find equivalent capacitance.

1/C_eq = 1/10 + 1/20 = 0.1 + 0.05 = 0.15
C_eq = 1/0.15 = 6.67 μF
Numerical Problem 4 (Parallel)

Three capacitors 5μF, 10μF, 15μF in parallel. Find C_eq.

C_eq = 5 + 10 + 15 = 30 μF
Numerical Problem 5 (Mixed)

Find equivalent capacitance between points: 6μF and 3μF in series, and this combination in parallel with 2μF.

Series part: 1/Cs = 1/6 + 1/3 = 0.1667 + 0.3333 = 0.5 ⇒ Cs = 2 μF
Parallel with 2μF: C_eq = 2 + 2 = 4 μF

Dielectric Effect & Dielectric Breakdown

Dielectric Constant (κ)

When a dielectric is inserted between plates, capacitance increases by factor κ.

C = κ C₀

where C₀ = capacitance without dielectric.

Dielectric reduces electric field inside: E = E₀/κ.

Dielectric Breakdown

If electric field exceeds dielectric strength, insulator becomes conductive – spark/arc occurs.

Example: Air breakdown ~ 3×10⁶ V/m. Capacitors have maximum voltage rating.

Numerical Problem 6

A parallel plate capacitor with air has capacitance 100pF. A dielectric of κ=5 is inserted. Find new capacitance.

C = κ × C₀ = 5 × 100 = 500 pF
