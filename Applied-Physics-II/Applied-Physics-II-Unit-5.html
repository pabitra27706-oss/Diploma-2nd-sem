Magnetic Field · Lorentz Force

Magnetic Field (B)

Origin: Moving charges (currents) and permanent magnets.

Units: Tesla (T) = N/(A·m). Also Gauss (1 G = 10⁻⁴ T).

Lorentz Force

Force on a charge q moving with velocity v in magnetic field B:

F = q (v × B)

Magnitude: F = q v B sinθ. Direction: perpendicular to both v and B (right-hand rule).

Numerical Problem 1

An electron (q = -1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C) moves at 2×10⁶ m/s perpendicular to 0.5T field. Find force.

F = q v B sin90° = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ × 2×10⁶ × 0.5 = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ × 10⁶ = 1.6×10⁻¹³ N

Biot-Savart Law · Straight Conductor · Circular Loop

Biot-Savart Law

dB = (μ₀/4π) (I dl × r̂) / r²

Magnetic field due to a current element.

Straight Conductor (long wire)

B = (μ₀ I) / (2π r) (at distance r from wire)

Circular Loop (center)

B = (μ₀ I) / (2R) (for N turns, multiply by N)

Magnetic Dipole

A current loop behaves as a magnetic dipole with dipole moment m = I × A (area).

Numerical Problem 2

A long straight wire carries 10A. Find B at 5cm from wire. (μ₀/4π = 10⁻⁷)

B = (μ₀ I)/(2π r) = (2×10⁻⁷ × 10) / (0.05) = 2×10⁻⁶ / 0.05 = 4×10⁻⁵ T
Numerical Problem 3

A circular coil of radius 10cm, 20 turns carries 2A. Find B at center.

B = (μ₀ N I)/(2R) = (4π×10⁻⁷ × 20 × 2) / (2×0.1) = (4π×10⁻⁷ × 40) / 0.2
= (160π×10⁻⁷)/0.2 = 800π×10⁻⁷ = 2.51×10⁻⁴ T

Force on Current Carrying Conductor · Torque on Coil

Force on a Current-Carrying Conductor

F = I (L × B) (for straight wire in uniform field)

Magnitude: F = B I L sinθ

Torque on Rectangular Coil in Magnetic Field

τ = N I A B sinθ (where θ is angle between normal to coil and B)

For N turns, area A, current I. This is the principle of moving coil galvanometer.

Numerical Problem 4

A 0.5m wire carries 3A perpendicular to 0.2T field. Find force.

F = B I L = 0.2 × 3 × 0.5 = 0.3 N

Electromagnetic Induction · Faraday's Laws

Concept

Changing magnetic flux through a coil induces an EMF (electromagnetic induction).

Faraday's Laws

First Law: EMF is induced whenever flux linked with circuit changes.

Second Law: Induced EMF = - rate of change of flux linkage.

ε = -N dΦ/dt

Moving Coil Galvanometer · Ammeter · Voltmeter

Principle, Construction & Working

Principle: Torque on current-carrying coil in magnetic field: τ = N B I A.

Construction: Coil suspended between poles of permanent magnet, with spring providing restoring torque.

Working: Current causes deflection θ ∝ I (since τ ∝ I and spring τ ∝ θ).

Conversion to Ammeter

Connect a low resistance shunt in parallel with galvanometer.

S = Ig G / (I - Ig) where G = galvanometer resistance, Ig = full scale current.

Conversion to Voltmeter

Connect a high resistance series with galvanometer.

R = V/Ig - G

Ammeter Conversion

A galvanometer of resistance 50Ω gives full deflection at 10mA. Convert to ammeter reading 5A. Find shunt resistance.

S = Ig G / (I - Ig) = (0.01 × 50) / (5 - 0.01) = 0.5 / 4.99 = 0.1002 Ω
Voltmeter Conversion

Same galvanometer (50Ω, 10mA) to measure 10V. Find series resistance.

R = V/Ig - G = 10/0.01 - 50 = 1000 - 50 = 950 Ω

Magnetic Materials: Dia · Para · Ferro

Diamagnetic

  • Weakly repelled by magnet
  • χ < 0, small negative susceptibility
  • Examples: Bi, Cu, Au, H₂O
  • No permanent dipole

Paramagnetic

  • Weakly attracted
  • χ > 0, small positive
  • Examples: Al, Pt, Na, O₂
  • Permanent dipoles align with field

Ferromagnetic

  • Strongly attracted
  • χ >> 0, large, depends on field
  • Examples: Fe, Ni, Co, Gd
  • Exhibit hysteresis, Curie temperature
