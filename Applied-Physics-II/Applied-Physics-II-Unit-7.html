Bohr's Atom Model · Energy Levels · Ionization & Excitation Potential

Bohr's Model
  • Electrons revolve in discrete orbits (energy levels) without radiating energy.
  • Angular momentum is quantized: mvr = nh/2π.
  • Energy is emitted/absorbed when electron jumps between levels: ΔE = hf.

Energy Levels

En = -13.6/n² eV (for hydrogen). Ground state (n=1): -13.6 eV.

Ionization Potential

Energy required to remove electron from ground state to infinity (n=∞). For H: 13.6 eV.

Excitation Potential

Energy required to raise electron from ground state to an excited state. e.g., 1st excitation potential = 10.2 eV (n=1 to n=2).

Numerical Problem

Find ionization potential of hydrogen in eV and joules.

Ionization potential = 13.6 eV
In joules: 13.6 × 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ = 2.176×10⁻¹⁸ J

X-Rays: Production, Types, Uses

Production (Coolidge Tube)

Electrons from hot cathode accelerated by high voltage strike metal target (W, Mo). X-rays produced via two processes:

Continuous X-rays

Due to deceleration of electrons (bremsstrahlung). Produces continuous spectrum up to a maximum energy corresponding to tube voltage.

Characteristic X-rays

When high-energy electrons knock out inner shell electrons, outer electrons fill vacancies emitting X-rays of specific wavelengths (characteristic of target material).

Soft vs Hard X-rays

Soft X-rays: Lower energy, longer wavelength (less penetrating).

Hard X-rays: Higher energy, shorter wavelength (highly penetrating).

Uses of X-rays
  • Medical imaging (radiography, CT scans)
  • Cancer therapy (radiation treatment)
  • Industrial inspection (crack detection)
  • Security (airport baggage scanners)
  • X-ray crystallography (structure determination)

Lasers: Spontaneous & Stimulated Emission · He-Ne Laser · Applications

Spontaneous Emission

Atoms in excited state randomly decay to lower state, emitting photons in random directions and phases (ordinary light).

Stimulated Emission

An incoming photon triggers an excited atom to emit a second photon identical in phase, direction, and polarization (laser light).

Laser Light Characteristics
  • Monochromatic (single wavelength)
  • Coherent (constant phase difference)
  • Directional (low divergence)
  • High intensity

He-Ne Laser

Gas laser: Helium-Neon mixture. Common red laser (632.8 nm). Continuous wave output.

Applications of Lasers

  • Medicine: surgery, eye correction
  • Communication: fiber optics
  • Industry: cutting, welding
  • Military: range finders
  • Entertainment: light shows
  • Barcode scanners

Fiber Optics: Introduction, Propagation, Applications

Optical Fibers

Thin, flexible glass/plastic fibers that transmit light using total internal reflection.

Mechanism of Light Propagation

Core (high refractive index) surrounded by cladding (lower index). Light guided by successive total internal reflections.

Acceptance angle and numerical aperture determine light-gathering ability.

Applications
  • Telecommunications (high bandwidth, low loss)
  • Medical endoscopy (minimally invasive surgery)
  • Sensors (temperature, pressure, strain)
  • Illumination and imaging

Nanoscience and Nanotechnology (Introduction)

Introduction

Nanoscience is the study of structures and materials at the nanoscale (1–100 nm).

Nanotechnology is the manipulation of matter at atomic/molecular scale to create new materials and devices.

Properties change dramatically at nanoscale due to quantum effects and high surface-to-volume ratio.

Examples: Nanoparticles, carbon nanotubes, quantum dots, nanocomposites.

Applications: Electronics, medicine (drug delivery), energy (solar cells), cosmetics.
