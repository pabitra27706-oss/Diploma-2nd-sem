🏗️ UNIT VI: LIFTING MACHINES PRIORITY #1 · Highest yield
⏱️ Study: 3-4 hrs 📈 Marks guarantee: 15–20 ⭐ Appears: Every year (2021-2025)
📌 What to cover (only high-yield parts):
  • ✔️ Law of machine: P = mW + C – find m & C from two data points (most repeated numerical)
  • ✔️ MA, VR, Efficiency relations: η = MA/VR, Max MA = 1/m, Max η = 1/(m·VR)
  • ✔️ Reversibility condition (η > 50%) & self-locking (η ≤ 50%)
  • ✔️ Velocity Ratio formulas: Screw jack (VR = 2πL/p), Wheel & Axle (VR = D/d), Differential wheel & axle (VR = 2D/(d₁-d₂))
  • ✔️ Numerical types: Given law P=0.2W+50, find MA, effort, load, max efficiency (2023,2025)
  • ✔️ Single purchase crab / worm & wheel VR (basic)

🔥 Appearance proof
2021 (diff. axle + law), 2022 (screw jack), 2023 (wheel & axle 5 parts), 2024 (VR + effort), 2025 (law + max MA).

📖 Tip: Learn to solve two linear equations (W₁,P₁) & (W₂,P₂) → get m, C. That alone fetches 6-8 marks.

📐 UNIT III: BEAM REACTIONS & EQUILIBRIUM PRIORITY #2 · Guaranteed numerical
⏱️ Study: 2.5 hrs 📈 Marks guarantee: 8–12 ⭐ Appears: 2021, 2022, 2024
🔧 Focus only on:
  • ✔️ Simply supported beam with point loads + UDL (full span or partial). Calculate reactions by ΣM=0, ΣV=0.
  • ✔️ Lami’s theorem (statement & simple concurrent force problem).
  • ✔️ Free body diagram concept (FBD).
  • ✔️ Types of supports (roller, hinge, fixed – theory question).
  • ❌ Skip complicated overhang with multiple UDLs (appears rarely).

✅ Exam trend: 2021: 4m span with 5,2,3kN → reactions. 2022: bridge 18m + lorry. 2024: 6m span + two point loads + UDL 12kN/m.

⚡ Smart trick: Take moments about one end; sum of loads = RA+RB. Always verify.

🧲 UNIT IV: FRICTION (Inclined & Horizontal) PRIORITY #3 · Regular 10-mark question
⏱️ Study: 3 hrs 📈 Marks guarantee: 10–12 ⭐ Appears: Every year (2021–2025)
🎯 Nail these problem types:
  • ✔️ Inclined plane: body of weight W, angle θ, find minimum P (parallel to plane) to prevent sliding down. Formula: P = W sinθ - μW cosθ (if motion down) OR P = W sinθ + μW cosθ (impending up).
  • ✔️ Horizontal plane: pull at angle θ & push at angle θ – compare to find μ and W.
  • ✔️ Angle of friction (φ) & angle of repose (θ = φ), relation tan φ = μ.
  • ✔️ Limiting friction definition & Coulomb's laws.

📅 Classic questions: 2021 (pull 180N/push 200N @30°), 2023 (500N on incline 25°, φ=20° find min P), 2025 (same pattern).

💡 Key reminder: Normal reaction changes when force is inclined. Draw FBD carefully.

📍 UNIT V: CENTROID (I-Section / Composite) PRIORITY #4 · Very scoring
⏱️ Study: 2 hrs 📈 Marks guarantee: 6–8 ⭐ Appears: 2021,2023,2024,2025
✍️ Only practice:
  • ✔️ Centroid of I-section (3 rectangles: top flange, web, bottom flange). Table method: Area, y (from base), A·y.
  • ✔️ Centroid of T-section, channel (basic composite shapes).
  • ✔️ Difference between centroid & centre of gravity (theory).
  • ❌ Avoid complex cut-out holes unless time permits (rare).

📐 Common data: Top flange 160×20, web 80×30, bottom flange 200×10 (mm) – appears in 2023,2024,2025 with slight dimension changes.

✅ Step: find reference axis (usually bottom), compute ΣAȳ/ΣA. Easy 6+ marks.

🧠 Quick Formula Card – "Easy Unit Power Pack"

⚙️ Lifting machines

MA = W / P  |  VR = effort distance / load distance

η = MA / VR  |  P = mW + C

Max MA = 1/m  |  Max η = 1/(m·VR)

Screw jack VR = 2πL / p

Wheel & axle VR = D/d

📐 Beam reactions

ΣFy = 0  →  RA + RB = total load

ΣMA = 0  →  RB·L = Σ(Wᵢ·xᵢ)

UDL: total force = w×L, acts at midspan

Lami: P/sinα = Q/sinβ = R/sinγ

🧲 Friction essentials

Fmax = μₛ × N  |  tan φ = μ, Angle of repose = φ

Inclined plane (P parallel): P = W sinθ ± μW cosθ

Horizontal pull: P cosθ = μ(W - P sinθ)

Horizontal push: P cosθ = μ(W + P sinθ)

📍 Centroid (I-section)

ȳ = Σ(Aᵢ·yᵢ) / ΣAᵢ   (from reference axis)

Rectangle: centroid = h/2 from base

Table method: Area, y, A·y → sum

Symmetry: x̄ = centre line
