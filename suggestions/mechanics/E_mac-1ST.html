📌 Unit 1: Force System & Basics Statics · Vectors · Resolution

🔹 State & explain Parallelogram law of forces. (Or Polygon law of forces)
2021, 2025 (MCQ) ★★★★★
🔹 Two forces 2P and P act on a particle. If first force is doubled & second increased by 12 kN, resultant direction unchanged. Find P.
2021, 2023, 2025 ★★★★★
🔹 Find resultant of concurrent coplanar forces (4 forces with directions – typical numerical).
2022, 2024 ★★★★☆
🔹 Principle of transmissibility of force & Bow’s notation.
2022, 2023 ★★★☆☆
🔹 Resolution of a force into rectangular components – numerical (horizontal & vertical).
2021, 2024 (MCQ basis) ★★★★☆
📐 Vector / Force basics
Resultant R = √(P² + Q² + 2PQ cosθ)
Direction tan α = (Q sinθ)/(P + Q cosθ)
Components: Fₓ = F cosθ , Fᵧ = F sinθ
⚖️ Parallelogram / Triangle law
Triangle law: R = P + Q (vector addition)
Polygon law: closing side gives resultant

🔹 State & explain Varignon’s theorem (principle of moments).

🔹 Replace a force by an equivalent force-couple system at a point.

2021 ★★★☆☆

🧮 Moment & Varignon
M = F × d (perpendicular distance)
Varignon: Moment of resultant = Σ moments of components
Couple moment: M = F × arm (independent of point)

🔹 State Lami’s theorem & application (force triangle for concurrent equilibrium).
2021, 2023 ★★★★☆
🔹 Ladder resting against smooth wall (5m, 60°, weight 900N) – find reactions / sliding condition.
2021 ★★★☆☆
🔹 Conditions of equilibrium for coplanar non-concurrent forces & free body diagram.
2022, 2024, 2025 ★★★★☆
🔹 Horizontal force P to hold block on smooth incline (30°).
2021, 2023 ★★★★☆
📌 Equilibrium equations
ΣFₓ = 0   ΣFᵧ = 0   ΣM = 0
Lami’s theorem: P/sinα = Q/sinβ = R/sinγ
📐 Beam reaction (simply supported)
RA = Σ(Wᵢ × dᵢ)/L , RB = total load – RA
UDL load = w × L (acts at mid-span)

🧲 Unit 4: Friction (Laws, Incline, Angle of Repose) μ · Limiting friction · Inclined plane

🔹 Body on rough horizontal plane: pull of 180N at 30° & push of 200N at 30° just moves it. Find weight & μ.
2021, 2024 ★★★★★
🔹 Weight 500N on rough inclined plane (25°), angle of friction = 20°. Determine minimum effort P (parallel to plane) to maintain equilibrium.
2023, 2025 ★★★★★
🔹 Define angle of friction, angle of repose & relation between them.
2022, 2023, 2024 ★★★★☆
🔹 Find friction force for given P = 20N, 50N, 60N (μₛ=0.5, μₖ=0.4) – static vs kinetic.
2023, 2025 ★★★★☆
🔹 Two smooth spheres in channel – find reactions (equilibrium + geometry).
2023, 2025 ★★★☆☆
🔁 Friction essentials
F_max = μₛ × N (limiting friction)
tan φ = μ (angle of friction)
Angle of repose θ = φ → tan θ = μ
📐 Inclined plane (P parallel to plane)
Motion up: P = W sinθ + μ W cosθ
Prevent sliding down: P = W sinθ − μ W cosθ (μ = tanφ)
Horizontal pull/push: P cosα = μ (W ∓ P sinα)

🏗️ Unit 6: Lifting Machines (Wheel & Axle, Screw jack, Law of machine) MA · VR · efficiency · reversibility

🔹 Law of machine P = mW + C. Find m, C from two observations (1500N with 100N, 2500N with 160N) – also MA & efficiency.
2021, 2023, 2025 ★★★★★
🔹 Differential wheel & axle: D=40cm, radii 15 & 10 cm, effort 100N, efficiency 75% → find load lifted.
2021, 2022 ★★★★☆
🔹 Simple screw jack: pitch = 10mm, handle length 500mm, effort 20N lifts 2.6kN. Find MA, VR, efficiency.
2022, 2023, 2024 ★★★★★
🔹 Condition for reversibility / self-locking machine. Prove η > 50% for reversibility.
2023, 2024, 2025 ★★★★☆
🔹 Wheel & axle: P = 0.2W + 50, D=600mm, r_axle=50mm. Find MA, effort for W=2000N, max efficiency, max MA, load for effort 520N.
2023, 2025 ★★★★★
🔹 Velocity ratio of simple wheel & axle, single/double purchase crab, worm & worm wheel.
2021, 2022, 2023, 2025 ★★★★☆
⚙️ Machine fundamentals
MA = W / P   VR = (effort distance)/(load distance)
η = MA / VR × 100%
Law of machine: P = mW + C → Max MA = 1/m , Max η = 1/(m·VR)
Self-locking: η ≤ 50%   Reversible: η > 50%
🔄 VR formulas (important)
Wheel & Axle: VR = D/d
Diff. Wheel & Axle: VR = 2D / (d₁ – d₂)
Screw jack: VR = 2πL / p (L = handle length, p = pitch)
Single purchase crab: VR = (lever length × T_pinion)/(R_axle × T_spur)

🏃 Unit 7: Motion in a Plane (Kinematics, Work, Power, Energy) Equations · Projectile · Work-Energy

🔹 Derive the distance travelled by a body in the nth second: Sₙ = u + a/2(2n-1).
2023, 2025 ★★★★★
🔹 Braking problem: car at 15 m/s, sees block 40m ahead, stops within 5m of block. Find retardation & time.
2025 ★★★★☆
🔹 Projectile: projected at 45°, 150 m/s. Calculate max height, range, time of flight.
2024 ★★★☆☆
🔹 Define work, power, energy with SI units & examples. (Potential & kinetic energy)
2023, 2024, 2025 ★★★★★
🔹 Pump power calculation: lift 18000 litres water to 12m in 20 min, efficiency 65% → find power.
2025 ★★★☆☆
🔹 Newton’s second law: P = mf, conservation of momentum (simple numerical).
2021, 2023 ★★★★☆
📈 Kinematic equations
v = u + at   s = ut + ½ at²   v² = u² + 2as
Distance in nth second: Sₙ = u + (a/2)(2n−1)
Centripetal force: F = mv²/r
💡 Work, Power, Energy
W = F × s × cosθ (Joule)
Power = Work/time = F·v (Watt)
KE = ½ mv²   PE = mgh
Pump power: P = (ṁ g h)/η (ṁ in kg/s)

📌 Exam strategy · 2024–2025 pattern & MCQ explanation

  • ⭐ 2025 updated pattern: MCQs in Group A now carry 2 marks each (1 mark for correct answer + 1 mark for valid explanation). Always write key reason/law/formula in one line.
  • 📖 High-frequency topics (must revise): Centroid of I-section, Law of machine (P=mW+C), Inclined plane friction, Beam reactions, Screw jack / Wheel & axle VR, Distance in nth second, Work-Energy definitions.
  • 🧮 Formula priority: MA, VR, η relations; Friction equations (inclined/horizontal); Beam equilibrium; Centroid composite formula; Kinematics equations.
  • ⌛ Time management: Attempt theory+derivation questions first (Varignon, Lami, reversibility). Numerical from friction, machine law, centroid carry heavy marks — practice tabular format.
  • ✅ For True/False & Fill-ups (2021-2023 pattern): Focus on SI units, angle of repose = angle of friction, MA max = 1/m, efficiency of self locking ≤ 50%.
  • ⚠️ Common mistakes: In friction problems, normal reaction changes when force inclined; in centroid, always measure reference line (bottom or top). In law of machine, take two observations to solve m & C.
