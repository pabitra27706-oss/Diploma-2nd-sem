📐 Key Formulas
SHM Displacement:y = A sin(ωt + φ)
Velocity in SHM:v = Aω cos(ωt + φ),  vmax = Aω
Acceleration in SHM:a = −ω²y,  amax = ω²A
Angular Frequency:ω = 2πf = 2π/T
Wave Equation:y = A sin(ωt − kx) = A sin 2π(t/T − x/λ)
Wave Speed:v = f λ = ω/k
Beat Frequency:fbeat = |f₁ − f₂|

🔑 Exam Tips for 2024–25 Pattern (Expected for 2026)

  • MCQs now carry 2 marks each: 1 mark for the correct option + 1 mark for a written explanation. Never skip the explanation – even a one-line justification can fetch that extra mark.
  • Time management: You have 2.5 hours. Spend ~25 minutes on the 10 MCQs, ~25 minutes per long question (you choose 5 out of 9–10). Reserve 10 minutes for final review.
  • Diagrams are compulsory: Questions on rectifiers, transistor circuits, ray diagrams, X-ray spectra, and energy level diagrams explicitly require neat, labelled diagrams. Practice drawing them from memory.
  • Prepare 5 easy units deeply: Units 1, 2, 3, 6, and 7 are highly predictable and mostly theoretical. Mastering just these 5 can secure 35+ marks (40 from long questions + aim for 15+ from MCQs).
  • Don't ignore Unit 4 & 5 for MCQs: These units contribute heavily to the 20-mark objective section. Revise basic formulas and definitions (resistivity, Kirchhoff, Biot-Savart, Faraday).
  • Numerical practice: SHM (vmax, amax), wave equation (find f, λ, v), lens formula, equivalent resistance/capacitance, X-ray λmin, Bohr transition energy – these are frequent and scoring.
  • Star ratings guide: ★★★★★ questions are almost guaranteed; ★★★★☆ are very likely; ★★★☆☆ and below are good to know but lower priority if time is short.
  • Write structured answers: For long questions, break your answer into clear sub-parts (a, b, c, d) as shown in the question paper. Use bullet points for comparisons and always end numericals with proper units.
