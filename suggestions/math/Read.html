📚 What to Study in Each Priority Unit

1 Determinants & Matrices
  • Evaluate 2×2 and 3×3 determinants
  • Minors, Cofactors, and finding inverse by adjoint method
  • Cramer's rule for 3 equations
  • Orthogonal matrix verification (AAᵀ = I)
  • det(kA) = kⁿdet(A), singular matrix condition
  • A·(adj A) = det(A)·I, skew‑symmetric properties
🕒 4–5 hours 📈 12–15 marks
2 Co‑ordinate Geometry
  • Collinearity of points (area = 0 or equal slopes)
  • Equation of straight line (all forms)
  • Angle between lines, parallel/perpendicular condition
  • Distance point↔line, distance between parallel lines
  • Circle: centre, radius, equation from conditions
  • Parabola: latus rectum, focus, directrix
  • Ellipse: eccentricity, foci, major axis
  • Cartesian ↔ Polar conversion
🕒 3–4 hours 📈 10–12 marks
3 Statistics & Probability
  • Mean, Median, Mode for grouped frequency
  • Standard Deviation & Variance (grouped data)
  • Probability of coins (at least one head, etc.)
  • Probability with two dice (sum, at least one '6')
  • Urn / ball problems (with/without replacement)
  • Addition rule P(A∪B), complementary events
  • Bayes' theorem & conditional probability
🕒 3–4 hours 📈 10–12 marks
4 Partial Differentiation
  • Compute ∂u/∂x and ∂u/∂y (treat other variable as constant)
  • Verify Euler's theorem (x·∂f/∂x + y·∂f/∂y = nf)
  • Prove identities like x·∂u/∂x + y·∂u/∂y = sin 2u
  • Use of implicit differentiation (tan u = expression)
🕒 1–2 hours 📈 8–10 marks

🧾 Essential Formula Card (Only the 4 Easy Units)

Determinant 2×2
|a b; c d| = ad − bc
Inverse (adjoint)
A⁻¹ = (1/det(A))·adj(A)
Cramer's Rule
x = det(Aₓ)/det(A) ; y = det(Aᵧ)/det(A)
Orthogonal Matrix
AAᵀ = I ⇒ A⁻¹ = Aᵀ
Collinear Points
Area = ½|x₁(y₂−y₃)+x₂(y₃−y₁)+x₃(y₁−y₂)| = 0
Line Slope & Eqns
m = (y₂−y₁)/(x₂−x₁)
y−y₁ = m(x−x₁) ; x/a+y/b=1
Distance Point→Line
|Ax₁+By₁+C|/√(A²+B²)
Circle Centre-Radius
(x−h)²+(y−k)²=r² ; x²+y²+2gx+2fy+c=0
Parabola / Ellipse
y²=4ax: LR=4a; ellipse: c²=a²−b², e=c/a
Median (Grouped)
L + [(N/2 − cf)/f] × h
Mean & SD
x̄ = Σfᵢxᵢ/N ; σ = √[Σfᵢ(xᵢ−x̄)²/N]
Probability Rules
P(Ē)=1−P(E) ; P(A∪B)=P(A)+P(B)−P(A∩B)
Euler's Theorem
x·∂f/∂x + y·∂f/∂y = n·f
Implicit Partial Diff
If tan u = f(x,y), differentiate both sides
Coins/Dice Total
n coins: 2ⁿ outcomes; 2 dice: 36
Bayes' Theorem
P(A|B)=[P(B|A)P(A)]/P(B)
📋 Latest Exam Pattern (2024–25): The paper has 10 MCQs (20–30 marks) and 5 long-answer questions (40 marks). In MCQs, you must explain each answer – 1 mark for the correct option and 1 mark for proper reasoning. Long questions are chosen from any 5 of the 10 available (covering all units). By preparing the 4 units above, you can easily answer 7–8 MCQs and 3–4 long questions, comfortably crossing 35 marks.

⏰ Time Management Tip: In a 1‑hour exam (2024), spend 15–18 minutes on MCQs (with explanations) and the remaining on long answers. Practise writing formulas first — many marks are awarded for correct steps even if the final answer slips.
