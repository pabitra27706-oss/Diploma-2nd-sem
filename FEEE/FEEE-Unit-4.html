Transformer and Machines - Overview

Introduction

Transformers and electrical machines (motors and generators) are fundamental electromechanical energy conversion devices.

  • Transformer: Static device, transfers electrical energy between circuits via electromagnetic induction.
  • Motor: Converts electrical energy into mechanical energy (motion).
  • Generator: Converts mechanical energy into electrical energy.

Transformers: Construction · Principle · EMF Equation

General Construction

Transformer consists of:

  • Core: Laminated silicon steel to reduce eddy currents
  • Windings: Primary and secondary coils (copper or aluminum)
  • Insulation: Between windings and core
  • Tank and bushings: For cooling and connections (in large transformers)
Primary (N₁) ⬅️ Core ➡️ Secondary (N₂)
Working Principle

Based on mutual induction. When AC applied to primary, alternating flux is set up in core. This flux links with secondary winding and induces EMF (Faraday's law).

Frequency remains same; voltage/current levels change based on turns ratio.

EMF Equation of Transformer

RMS EMF per turn: E = 4.44 f Φm N

Where: f = frequency, Φm = maximum flux in core (Wb), N = number of turns

E₁ = 4.44 f Φm N₁ (primary EMF)
E₂ = 4.44 f Φm N₂ (secondary EMF)

Transformation Ratio (K)

K = E₂ / E₁ = N₂ / N₁

K < 1 : Step-down
K > 1 : Step-up

Voltage/Current Relation

V₂ / V₁ = N₂ / N₁ = K (ideal)

I₂ / I₁ = N₁ / N₂ = 1/K (for ideal, neglecting losses)

Types of Transformers
  • Power transformer: Used in transmission network
  • Distribution transformer: Used for local distribution
  • Instrument transformer: CT (current transformer), PT (potential transformer)
  • Isolation transformer: 1:1 ratio for safety

Auto Transformers

What is an Auto Transformer?

An auto transformer has a single continuous winding with a tap point. Part of winding is common to both primary and secondary.

Advantages: Smaller, cheaper, higher efficiency than two-winding transformer for same KVA.

Disadvantage: No electrical isolation between primary and secondary.

Voltage ratio: V₁/V₂ = N₁/N₂
Currents: I₁·N₁ = I₂·N₂ + (I₂ - I₁)·N₂? simpler: I₁/I₂ = N₂/N₁

Motors: Construction · Working · Types · Applications

Construction and Working Principle

Electric motor converts electrical energy to mechanical energy using the principle that a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field experiences a force (F = BIL).

Basic parts: Stator (stationary part), Rotor (rotating part), Commutator (in DC motors), Brushes, Bearings.

Types of Motors and Applications

DC Motors

  • Series motor: High starting torque (cranes, hoists)
  • Shunt motor: Constant speed (lathes, fans)
  • Compound motor: Intermediate characteristics (elevators, conveyors)
  • PMDC (permanent magnet): Small toys, automotive

AC Motors

  • Induction motor (squirrel cage): Most common industrial motor (pumps, compressors, fans)
  • Slip ring induction motor: High starting torque (cranes, lifts)
  • Single-phase induction motor: Domestic appliances (fans, washing machines, ACs)
  • Synchronous motor: Constant speed, power factor correction

Special Motors

  • Stepper motor: Precise positioning (robotics, printers)
  • Servo motor: Closed-loop control (CNC, robotics)
  • BLDC motor: Efficient, long life (EVs, fans)

Simple Problems on Transformer & Machines

Problem 1: A 50 Hz transformer has 500 turns on primary and 100 turns on secondary. Maximum flux in core is 0.02 Wb. Calculate primary and secondary induced EMF.

E₁ = 4.44 f Φm N₁ = 4.44 × 50 × 0.02 × 500
= 4.44 × 50 × 10 = 4.44 × 500 = 2220 V
E₂ = 4.44 f Φm N₂ = 4.44 × 50 × 0.02 × 100
= 4.44 × 50 × 2 = 4.44 × 100 = 444 V
Turns ratio K = N₂/N₁ = 100/500 = 0.2 (step-down)

Problem 2: A single phase transformer has 600 primary turns and 150 secondary turns. Primary is connected to 2300 V, 50 Hz supply. Find secondary voltage and transformation ratio.

K = N₂/N₁ = 150/600 = 0.25
V₂ = K × V₁ = 0.25 × 2300 = 575 V

Problem 3: An auto transformer supplies a load of 10 kW at 200 V from a 400 V supply. Neglecting losses, find the current in the common winding.

Load current I₂ = P / V₂ = 10000 / 200 = 50 A
Input current I₁ = P / V₁ = 10000 / 400 = 25 A
In auto transformer, common winding current = I₂ - I₁ = 50 - 25 = 25 A

Problem 4: A 230V DC shunt motor draws an armature current of 30A. Armature resistance is 0.5Ω. Find back EMF and power developed.

Back EMF Eb = V - IaRa = 230 - 30×0.5 = 230 - 15 = 215 V
Power developed = Eb × Ia = 215 × 30 = 6450 W (6.45 kW)

Problem 5: A 4-pole, 50 Hz induction motor runs at 1440 rpm. Find slip and rotor frequency.

Synchronous speed Ns = 120f/P = 120×50/4 = 1500 rpm
Slip s = (Ns - N) / Ns = (1500 - 1440)/1500 = 60/1500 = 0.04 (4%)
Rotor frequency fr = s·f = 0.04 × 50 = 2 Hz
