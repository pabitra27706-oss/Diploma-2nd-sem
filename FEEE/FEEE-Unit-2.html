EMF · Current · Potential Difference · Power & Energy

Electromotive Force (EMF)

EMF is the energy supplied by a source per unit charge. Unit: Volt (V). It is the potential difference across the terminals when no current flows.

Current (I)

Rate of flow of charge. I = dQ/dt. Unit: Ampere (A). Direction: conventional (positive to negative).

Potential Difference (PD)

Voltage across two points when current flows. PD = I·R. Less than EMF due to internal resistance.

Power (P)

Rate of energy transfer. P = V·I = I²R = V²/R. Unit: Watt (W).

Energy (E)

Total work done. E = P·t = V·I·t. Unit: Joule (J) or kWh.

Problem: A 12V battery supplies 2A current for 5 minutes. Calculate power and energy delivered.

Solution:
Power P = V·I = 12V × 2A = 24 W
Time t = 5 min = 300 s
Energy E = P·t = 24W × 300s = 7200 J (or 7.2 kJ)

Magnetic Circuits · MMF · Hysteresis · Reluctance · B-H

Key Magnetic Quantities
  • MMF (Magnetomotive Force) = N·I (ampere-turns). Drives flux.
  • Magnetic Force – force on a moving charge in magnetic field: F = qvB sinθ.
  • Permeability (μ) – measure of how easily magnetic lines pass through material. μ = B/H.
  • Reluctance (S) – opposition to magnetic flux. S = l / (μ·A). Analogy to resistance.
  • Leakage factor – ratio of total flux to useful flux (λ = Φtotal / Φuseful).
B-H Curve & Hysteresis Loop

B-H curve shows relationship between magnetic flux density (B) and magnetic field strength (H).

↰ B ↑ / H →

Hysteresis loop – when material is magnetized in cycles, B lags behind H. Area of loop = hysteresis loss (heat).

Hysteresis loss ∝ area of loop × frequency.

Eddy current loss – circulating currents induced in core; reduced by laminations.

Hysteresis loss

Ph = η·Bmax1.6·f·V

Eddy current loss

Pe = ke·Bmax2·f2·t2·V
Analogy: Electric vs Magnetic Circuits
Electric · EMF (V) ↔ Magnetic · MMF (NI)
Electric · Current (I) ↔ Magnetic · Flux (Φ)
Electric · Resistance (R) ↔ Magnetic · Reluctance (S)
Electric · Conductivity (σ) ↔ Magnetic · Permeability (μ)

Electromagnetic Induction · Faraday's Laws · Lenz's Law

Faraday's Laws

First law: Whenever magnetic flux linked with a circuit changes, an EMF is induced.

Second law: Induced EMF magnitude = rate of change of flux linkage. e = -N dΦ/dt

Lenz's Law

The induced EMF opposes the change in flux that produces it (negative sign in Faraday's equation).

e = - N dΦ/dt
Self & Mutual Inductance

Self inductance (L): property of a coil to induce EMF in itself due to change in its own current. e = -L di/dt

Mutual inductance (M): EMF induced in one coil due to change in current in another coil. e₂ = -M di₁/dt

Types of Induced EMF · Self/Mutual Inductance · Energy Stored

Dynamically Induced EMF

Conductor moves in a stationary magnetic field. e = B·l·v sinθ (generator action).

Statically Induced EMF

Magnetic field changes, conductor stationary. e = N dΦ/dt (transformer action).

Energy stored in magnetic circuit

Energy stored in an inductor: W = ½ L I² (Joules).

Energy density in magnetic field: u = ½ B²/μ (J/m³).

Problem: A coil of self-inductance 5H carries a current of 2A. Calculate energy stored.

Solution:
W = ½ L I² = ½ × 5 × (2)² = ½ × 5 × 4 = 10 J

Problem: The mutual inductance between two coils is 2H. If current in primary changes from 3A to 5A in 0.1s, find induced EMF in secondary.

Solution:
e = M · di/dt = 2H × (5-3)/0.1 = 2 × 2/0.1 = 2 × 20 = 40 V

Fleming's Left Hand & Right Hand Rules

Left Hand Rule (Motor)

Thumb → Motion (Force), Forefinger → Field (N to S), Middle finger → Current (conventional).

Used for motors: force on current-carrying conductor in magnetic field.

Right Hand Rule (Generator)

Thumb → Motion, Forefinger → Field, Middle finger → Induced EMF (current).

Used for generators: induced EMF when conductor moves in field.

Mnemonic

LEFT for Locomotion (motor). RIGHT for Result (generator).

Additional Practice Problems

Problem 1: A magnetic circuit has MMF of 500 AT and reluctance of 2×10⁵ AT/Wb. Find flux.

Φ = MMF / Reluctance = 500 / (2×10⁵) = 2.5×10⁻³ Wb (2.5 mWb)

Problem 2: Calculate hysteresis loss per cycle if area of B-H loop is 400 J/m³ and volume of core is 0.01 m³ at 50 Hz.

Loss per cycle = area × volume = 400 J/m³ × 0.01 m³ = 4 J/cycle
Total loss = 4 J/cycle × 50 Hz = 200 W

Problem 3: A conductor of length 0.5m moves at 20 m/s perpendicular to magnetic field of 0.8T. Find dynamically induced EMF.

e = B·l·v = 0.8 × 0.5 × 20 = 8 V
