Centroid & Centre of Gravity - Concept

Definitions

Centroid: The geometric center of a plane figure (point where the entire area is assumed to be concentrated). It depends only on shape.

Centre of Gravity (CG): The point where the entire weight of a body is assumed to act. For uniform bodies, CG coincides with centroid.

Centroid of Plane Figures

△

Triangle

Centroid at intersection of medians, h/3 from base

□

Rectangle

Centroid at center (b/2, h/2)

○

Circle

Centroid at center

◡

Semicircle

y̅ = 4r/(3π) from diameter

◩

Quadrant

x̅ = y̅ = 4r/(3π) from corner

Uniform Triangular Lamina

Centroid at a distance h/3 from base (for altitude h).

Uniform Rectangular Lamina

Centroid at (b/2, h/2).

Uniform Circular Lamina

Centroid at center of circle.

Uniform Semi-circular Lamina

Centroid distance from base = 4r/(3π).

Uniform Quadrant of a Circle

Centroid at x̅ = y̅ = 4r/(3π) from the two straight edges.

Example: Semicircle

Find centroid of a semicircular lamina of radius 100mm from the base.

y̅ = 4r/(3π) = 4×100/(3×3.14) = 400/9.42 = 42.46 mm

Centroid of Composite Sections

General Formula

For composite areas, centroid coordinates:

x̅ = Σ(Aᵢ × xᵢ) / ΣAᵢ
y̅ = Σ(Aᵢ × yᵢ) / ΣAᵢ

where Aᵢ = area of each part, (xᵢ, yᵢ) = centroid of that part.

T-Section

Divide into two rectangles (flange and web). Find overall centroid.

Angle Section (L)

Equal and unequal legs. Two rectangles.

I-Section

Three rectangles: top flange, web, bottom flange.

Channel Section

Similar to I but flanges on same side? Actually channel has web and two flanges.

Z-Section

Three rectangles arranged in Z shape.

Cut-out Sections

Subtract area of hole (negative area).

Example: T-Section

A T-section has flange 100×20mm, web 80×20mm (total height 100mm). Find centroid from base.

Divide into two rectangles:
Part 1 (flange): A₁ = 100×20 = 2000 mm², y₁ = 100 - 10 = 90mm (from base)
Part 2 (web): A₂ = 80×20 = 1600 mm², y₂ = 40mm (center of web from base)
ΣA = 3600 mm²
ΣA·y = 2000×90 + 1600×40 = 180000 + 64000 = 244000
y̅ = 244000 / 3600 = 67.78 mm from base.
Example: Unequal Angle Section

Angle 150×100×10mm (legs thickness 10mm). Find centroid from left and bottom.

Two rectangles:
Vertical leg: 150×10, area=1500, centroid x=5, y=75
Horizontal leg: 100×10, area=1000, but careful: it extends from bottom, so its centroid x=50, y=5
But horizontal leg length 100, but part of it overlaps? Actually angle: vertical leg 150 tall, 10 wide; horizontal leg 100 long, 10 thick, attached at bottom. So coordinates:
A₁=1500, x₁=5, y₁=75
A₂=1000, x₂=50 (since from left edge of horizontal), y₂=5
ΣA=2500
x̅ = (1500×5 + 1000×50)/2500 = (7500 + 50000)/2500 = 57500/2500 = 23 mm
y̅ = (1500×75 + 1000×5)/2500 = (112500 + 5000)/2500 = 117500/2500 = 47 mm
Example: Cut-out Section

A rectangular plate 200×150mm has a circular hole of diameter 50mm at center. Find centroid of remaining area.

Full rectangle area = 200×150 = 30000 mm², centroid at (100,75)
Hole area (negative) = π×25² = 1963.5 mm², centroid at (100,75)
Since hole is at same centroid, overall centroid remains at (100,75).
(If hole is offset, use negative area method.)
Built-up Section

Two channel sections (each area 2500 mm², CG at 20mm from back) placed back-to-back 200mm apart. Find overall CG.

By symmetry, y̅ is at mid-height of channels. x̅: from left edge, first channel CG at 20, second at 200-20=180.
ΣA·x = 2500×20 + 2500×180 = 50000 + 450000 = 500000
ΣA = 5000, x̅ = 500000/5000 = 100 mm (centerline)

⬛

🧱

🥫

CG of Composite Solids (Concept only)

Method

For composite solids (max two simple solids), the CG coordinates are found using:

x̅ = Σ(Vᵢ × xᵢ) / ΣVᵢ
y̅ = Σ(Vᵢ × yᵢ) / ΣVᵢ
z̅ = Σ(Vᵢ × zᵢ) / ΣVᵢ

where Vᵢ = volume of each part, (xᵢ, yᵢ, zᵢ) = CG of that part.

Note: Problems not required as per syllabus, only conceptual understanding.

Conceptual Example

A solid cylinder of radius r and height h has a hemisphere of same radius attached to its top. The CG will lie on the axis, somewhere between the cylinder's CG (h/2) and the hemisphere's CG (3r/8 from base of hemisphere).

Formula Quick Reference

Triangle: h/3 from base
Semicircle: 4r/(3π)
Quadrant: 4r/(3π)
Composite: x̅ = ΣAx / ΣA
Cut-out: negative area
Solids: CG at center
