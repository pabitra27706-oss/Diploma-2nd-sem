Concept of Engineering Mechanics

Statics & Dynamics

Statics: Study of bodies at rest under forces in equilibrium.

Dynamics: Study of bodies in motion. It has two branches: Kinematics (motion without considering forces) and Kinetics (motion with forces).

Space, Time, Mass

Space: geometric region where events occur.
Time: measure of sequence of events.
Mass: quantity of matter.

Particle, Rigid Body, Flexible Body

Particle: mass with negligible size.
Rigid body: deformation negligible.
Flexible body: deforms under load.

Scalar & Vector Quantities

Scalar: magnitude only (mass, time, temperature).

Vector: magnitude and direction (force, velocity, acceleration).

Addition & Subtraction of Vectors

Vectors are added using parallelogram law or triangle law. Subtraction: A - B = A + (-B).

Units: Basic, Derived, SI

Basic SI units: meter (m), kilogram (kg), second (s), ampere (A), kelvin (K), mole (mol), candela (cd).

Derived units: Newton (N = kg·m/s²), Joule (J = N·m), Pascal (Pa = N/m²).

Force: Definition, Unit, Representation

What is Force?

Force is an action that tends to change the state of rest or motion of a body. It is a vector quantity.

Unit: Newton (N) in SI system.

Bow's Notation

Method to label forces in a space diagram using capital letters. Forces are named by letters on either side (e.g., AB, BC). Used in graphical analysis.

Representation as Vector

Force represented by a line: length = magnitude, arrow = direction, starting point = point of application.

Characteristics & Effects of Force
  • Magnitude
  • Direction (line of action)
  • Point of application
  • Effects: may cause motion, deformation, or both.
Principle of Transmissibility of Force

The effect of a force on a rigid body remains unchanged if the force is moved along its line of action (sliding vector).

Force Systems & Classification

Classification of Force Systems
Coplanar (forces in same plane)
Non-coplanar (forces in different planes)
Concurrent (lines of action meet at one point)
Non-concurrent (do not meet)
Parallel (forces parallel to each other)
Collinear (forces along same line)
Coplanar Force System

Emphasis on Coplanar: All forces lie in the same plane. Most common in 2D problems.

  • Coplanar Concurrent
  • Coplanar Parallel
  • Coplanar Non-concurrent

Coplanar Concurrent Force System · Composition

Laws of Force Composition
  • Parallelogram Law: If two forces are represented by adjacent sides of a parallelogram, the diagonal represents resultant.
  • Triangle Law: If two forces are represented by sides of a triangle taken in order, the third side represents resultant.
  • Polygon Law: For multiple forces, sides of polygon represent forces, closing side gives resultant.
Example: Parallelogram Law

Two forces of 50N and 80N act at an angle of 60°. Find resultant magnitude and direction.

R = √(P² + Q² + 2PQ cosθ)
= √(50² + 80² + 2×50×80×cos60°)
= √(2500 + 6400 + 8000×0.5)
= √(8900 + 4000) = √12900 = 113.6 N
Direction α = tan⁻¹( (Q sinθ)/(P + Q cosθ) )
= tan⁻¹( (80×0.866)/(50 + 80×0.5) ) = tan⁻¹(69.28/90) = 37.6°
Graphical Method & Vector Diagram

Using Bow's notation and space diagram, draw force polygon to scale. The closing side gives resultant.

F₁ F₂ F₃ R

Problem: Three forces 30N, 40N, and 50N act at a point. First two are at 90° to each other, and the third is at 30° from the 40N force. Find resultant using polygon law.

Analytical approach:
Resolve forces into x and y components:
F₁ (30N along x): Fx₁=30, Fy₁=0
F₂ (40N along y): Fx₂=0, Fy₂=40
F₃ (50N at 30° from 40N force, i.e., from y-axis): angle from x-axis = 60°
Fx₃ = 50 cos60° = 25, Fy₃ = 50 sin60° = 43.3
ΣFx = 30+0+25 = 55N
ΣFy = 0+40+43.3 = 83.3N
R = √(55² + 83.3²) = √(3025 + 6939) = √9964 = 99.8 N
θ = tan⁻¹(83.3/55) = 56.5° from x-axis.

Resolution of Forces · Orthogonal Components

Orthogonal Components

A force F at angle θ to x-axis has components:

Fx = F cosθ
Fy = F sinθ
Example: Resolution

A force of 100N acts at 40° to the horizontal. Find its horizontal and vertical components.

Fx = 100 cos40° = 100 × 0.766 = 76.6 N
Fy = 100 sin40° = 100 × 0.643 = 64.3 N

Problem: Resolve a force of 250N into two components: one along a line making 30° and the other along a line making 120° with the force direction.

Using parallelogram law components:
Component along 30°: F₁ = F sin(120°)/sin(150°) = 250 × sin120°/sin150°
sin120° = 0.866, sin150° = 0.5
F₁ = 250 × 0.866/0.5 = 433 N
Component along 120°: F₂ = F sin30°/sin150° = 250 × 0.5/0.5 = 250 N
Simple Problem: Resultant using Resolution

Two forces 60N at 30° and 80N at 60° (both from x-axis). Find resultant.

F₁: Fx₁=60cos30=51.96, Fy₁=60sin30=30
F₂: Fx₂=80cos60=40, Fy₂=80sin60=69.28
ΣFx=91.96, ΣFy=99.28
R = √(91.96²+99.28²) = √(8457+9857) = √18314 = 135.3 N
θ = tan⁻¹(99.28/91.96) = 47.2°
