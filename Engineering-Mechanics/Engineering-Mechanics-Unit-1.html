Moment of a Force · Definition & Significance

Definition

The moment of a force about a point is the measure of its tendency to cause rotation about that point.

M = F × d

where F = force magnitude, d = perpendicular distance from point to line of action of force (moment arm).

Unit: Newton-meter (Nm) or kNm.

↓ F
d
Physical Significance

Moment causes rotation. Examples: opening a door (force at handle), using a wrench, seesaw.

Sign convention: Anticlockwise moments are usually taken as positive (+), clockwise as negative (-).

Resultant of Parallel Forces

For a system of parallel forces, the resultant is the algebraic sum of forces. Its position is found by taking moments about any point: R × d = Σ(F × x).

Example: Parallel Forces

Two parallel forces: 20N upward at 0m, 30N downward at 2m. Find resultant magnitude and position.

R = 20 - 30 = -10N (10N downward)
Take moments about 0: ΣM = (20×0) + (-30×2) = -60 Nm
R × d = -60 ⇒ (-10) × d = -60 ⇒ d = 6m (from origin, on the side of the 30N force)

Problem: A force of 50N acts at 30° to horizontal at point (2,3). Find moment about origin.

Resolve force: Fx = 50 cos30° = 43.3N, Fy = 50 sin30° = 25N
Moment about origin = Fx × y + Fy × x (with sign)
= 43.3 × 3 + 25 × 2 = 129.9 + 50 = 179.9 Nm (anticlockwise positive)

Varignon's Theorem

Statement

Varignon's Theorem: The moment of the resultant of a system of forces about any point is equal to the algebraic sum of the moments of the individual forces about the same point.

MR = Σ Mi
Example: Varignon's Theorem

Two forces: 30N at (0,0) along +x, and 40N at (0,0) at 60° from x-axis. Find moment of resultant about point (2,0) and verify by summing moments.

Resultant: Rx = 30 + 40cos60° = 30+20=50N, Ry = 40sin60°=34.64N
R = √(50²+34.64²)=60.8N
Moment of R about (2,0): need perpendicular distance – easier to use components.
Using Varignon: moment of each force about (2,0)
For 30N (horizontal at y=0): lever arm=0 → M1=0
For 40N: components: 40cos60°=20N (horiz), 40sin60°=34.64N (vert)
M2 = 20×0 (horizontal through y=0) + 34.64×2 = 69.28 Nm
Sum = 69.28 Nm
Direct: resultant components: 50N horiz, 34.64N vert at (0,0)
Moment = 50×0 + 34.64×2 = 69.28 Nm ✓

Couples: Definition & Physical Significance

Definition of a Couple

A couple consists of two equal, parallel, and opposite forces separated by a perpendicular distance.

Moment of couple = F × d

where F = magnitude of one force, d = perpendicular distance between them.

↑ F
↓ F
d
↑ F
↓ F
Physical Significance

A couple produces pure rotation without translation. Examples: turning a steering wheel, using a screwdriver, opening a bottle cap.

Equivalent Couples & Resultant of Coplanar Couples

Equivalent Couples

Two couples are equivalent if they produce the same moment (magnitude and direction). The forces and distances can differ as long as F×d is same.

Resultant of Coplanar Couples

The resultant of any number of coplanar couples is a single couple whose moment equals the algebraic sum of individual couple moments.

CR = Σ Ci
Example: Resultant of Couples

Three couples: 20 Nm clockwise, 15 Nm anticlockwise, 30 Nm clockwise. Find resultant couple.

Take clockwise as positive: +20 -15 +30 = 35 Nm clockwise

Replacement of a Force by an Equal Parallel Force + Couple

Principle

Any force acting at a point can be replaced by an equal force acting at any other point, together with a couple that accounts for the moment of the original force about that point.

The couple moment = F × d, where d is perpendicular distance between original line of action and new point.

Example: Force Replacement

A 100N force acts vertically downward at point (0,2). Replace it by an equivalent system at the origin (0,0).

At origin: place a 100N downward force.
Moment of original force about origin = F × d = 100 × 2 = 200 Nm (clockwise)
So add a clockwise couple of 200 Nm.
Equivalent: 100N downward at origin + 200 Nm couple (clockwise).

Problem: Replace a 50N force acting at 30° to horizontal at point (3,4) by an equivalent force-couple system at the origin.

Force components: Fx = 50 cos30° = 43.3N, Fy = 50 sin30° = 25N
Moment about origin = Fx × y + Fy × x = 43.3×4 + 25×3 = 173.2 + 75 = 248.2 Nm (anticlockwise)
Equivalent: at origin: 43.3N i + 25N j force, plus a couple of 248.2 Nm anticlockwise.

More Simple Problems

Problem 1: Find the moment of a force of 80N acting at 60° to horizontal at point (4,0) about point (1,2).

Fx = 80 cos60° = 40N, Fy = 80 sin60° = 69.28N
Position vector from (1,2) to (4,0): dx=3, dy=-2
Moment = Fx×dy - Fy×dx? Actually M = (r × F) = r_x·F_y - r_y·F_x
= 3×69.28 - (-2)×40 = 207.84 + 80 = 287.84 Nm (sign depends on convention)

Problem 2: Two forces of 30N and 40N act at right angles to each other at a point. Find the moment of their resultant about a point 2m away perpendicular to the resultant's direction.

Resultant magnitude = √(30²+40²) = 50N
Moment = Resultant × distance = 50 × 2 = 100 Nm
