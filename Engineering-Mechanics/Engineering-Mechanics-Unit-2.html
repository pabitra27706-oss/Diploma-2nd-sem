Equilibrium of Concurrent Force System

Conditions for Equilibrium

For a concurrent force system, equilibrium occurs when the resultant force is zero.

ΣFx = 0, ΣFy = 0
Lami's Theorem

If three concurrent forces are in equilibrium, each force is proportional to the sine of the angle between the other two forces.

P / sinα = Q / sinβ = R / sinγ

where α, β, γ are angles opposite to forces P, Q, R respectively.

Example: Lami's Theorem

A weight of 500N is suspended by two strings making angles of 30° and 60° with the horizontal. Find tensions.

The three forces: T₁, T₂, and 500N weight are in equilibrium.
Angles: between T₁ and T₂ = 90° (since 30+60=90, but careful: between the two strings is 90°)
Angle opposite to 500N = 90°, opposite to T₁ = 150°, opposite to T₂ = 120°
Using Lami: T₁/sin150° = T₂/sin120° = 500/sin90°
sin150°=0.5, sin120°=0.866, sin90°=1
T₁ = 500 × 0.5 = 250N
T₂ = 500 × 0.866 = 433N
Triangle Law & Polygon Law of Equilibrium

Triangle Law: If three forces are in equilibrium, they can be represented by the sides of a triangle taken in order.

Polygon Law: If multiple forces are in equilibrium, they form a closed polygon.

Concept of Free Body Diagram (FBD)

What is FBD?

A Free Body Diagram is a sketch of a body isolated from its surroundings showing all forces acting on it (applied loads, reactions, weights).

↑ R ↓ W ← P

Block on floor with pull

↑ N ↓ mg → F

FBD: Normal, weight, force

Steps to draw FBD:

  1. Isolate the body
  2. Show all external forces (direction and magnitude if known)
  3. Include weight if significant
  4. Show reaction forces from supports/contacts

Equilibrium of Co-planar Non-concurrent Forces

Conditions of Equilibrium

For non-concurrent forces, three conditions must be satisfied:

ΣFx = 0, ΣFy = 0, ΣM = 0 (about any point)
Parallel Forces (Like and Unlike)

Like parallel forces: act in same direction.
Unlike parallel forces: act in opposite directions.

For equilibrium of parallel forces: ΣF = 0 and ΣM = 0.

Example: Parallel Forces

A beam of length 6m carries a 20N downward force at 2m from left and a 30N downward force at 5m from left. Find reactions at ends (simple supports).

Let R₁ at left, R₂ at right.
ΣFy = 0: R₁ + R₂ = 20 + 30 = 50N
ΣM about left: 20×2 + 30×5 - R₂×6 = 0
40 + 150 = 6R₂ ⇒ 190 = 6R₂ ⇒ R₂ = 31.67N
R₁ = 50 - 31.67 = 18.33N

Beam Reactions · Simple Problems

Simply Supported Beam with Point Loads

For a simply supported beam, reactions are found using ΣFy=0 and ΣM=0.

Example 1: Point Loads

A simply supported beam of length 8m has point loads: 10kN at 2m from left, 20kN at 5m from left. Find reactions.

ΣM about left: 10×2 + 20×5 - R₂×8 = 0
20 + 100 = 8R₂ ⇒ 120 = 8R₂ ⇒ R₂ = 15 kN
ΣFy: R₁ + 15 = 10 + 20 ⇒ R₁ = 30 - 15 = 15 kN
Example 2: UDL

A simply supported beam of length 6m carries a UDL of 5 kN/m over its entire span. Find reactions.

Total load = 5 × 6 = 30 kN (acts at center, 3m from each end)
By symmetry, R₁ = R₂ = 30/2 = 15 kN each
Example 3: Overhanging Beam

A beam with overhang: length 8m (supports at 0 and 6m), overhang 2m on right. Point load 10kN at free end (8m). Find reactions.

Let R₁ at left (0m), R₂ at 6m.
ΣM about left: 10×8 - R₂×6 = 0 ⇒ 80 = 6R₂ ⇒ R₂ = 13.33 kN
ΣFy: R₁ + 13.33 = 10 ⇒ R₁ = -3.33 kN (downward)
So R₁ acts downward (3.33 kN), R₂ = 13.33 kN upward.
Example 4: Combination of Point Load and UDL

Simply supported beam 5m long. UDL 4 kN/m over first 3m, point load 8kN at 4m from left. Find reactions.

UDL total = 4×3 = 12 kN, acts at 1.5m from left.
ΣM about left: 12×1.5 + 8×4 - R₂×5 = 0
18 + 32 = 5R₂ ⇒ 50 = 5R₂ ⇒ R₂ = 10 kN
ΣFy: R₁ + 10 = 12 + 8 ⇒ R₁ = 20 - 10 = 10 kN

Practice Problem: A simply supported beam of length 7m has a UDL of 3 kN/m over the entire span and a point load of 12 kN at 2.5m from left. Find support reactions.

Total UDL load = 3×7 = 21 kN (acts at 3.5m from left)
ΣM about left: 21×3.5 + 12×2.5 - R₂×7 = 0
73.5 + 30 = 7R₂ ⇒ 103.5 = 7R₂ ⇒ R₂ = 14.79 kN
R₁ = 21 + 12 - 14.79 = 18.21 kN
