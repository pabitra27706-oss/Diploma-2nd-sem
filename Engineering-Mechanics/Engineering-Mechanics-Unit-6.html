Motion Equations (with deduction)

For constant acceleration a:

v = u + at
s = ut + ½at²
v² = u² + 2as

Deduction: From a = dv/dt → ∫dv = ∫a dt → v = u + at. Then ds/dt = v → ∫ds = ∫(u+at)dt → s = ut + ½at². Eliminating t gives v² = u² + 2as.

Newton's Second Law

Force = mass × acceleration

P = ma

where P = net force, m = mass, a = acceleration.

Momentum

Momentum = mass × velocity

p = mv

SI unit: kg·m/s

Conservation of Momentum

In absence of external forces, total momentum of a system remains constant.

m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂

Numerical Problem 1

A car starts from rest and accelerates at 2 m/s² for 5s. Find velocity and distance covered.

u=0, a=2 m/s², t=5s
v = u + at = 0 + 2×5 = 10 m/s
s = ut + ½at² = 0 + ½×2×25 = 25 m
Numerical Problem 2 (Momentum)

A 2kg ball moving at 3 m/s collides with a 3kg stationary ball. After collision, they move together. Find common velocity.

Initial momentum = 2×3 + 3×0 = 6 kg·m/s
Final mass = 5 kg
v = 6/5 = 1.2 m/s

Curvilinear Motion

Angular Motion Concepts
  • Angular displacement (θ): angle swept by radius vector (radians).
  • Angular velocity (ω): ω = dθ/dt (rad/s).
  • Angular acceleration (α): α = dω/dt (rad/s²).
Relation between Linear & Angular Quantities
v = r ω
at = r α

where r = radius of circular path, v = linear velocity, at = tangential acceleration.

Centripetal Force

For circular motion, centripetal force acts towards center.

Fc = mv²/r = m r ω²

Deduction: From Newton's 2nd, F = ma, and centripetal acceleration ac = v²/r = r ω².

Centrifugal Force

Pseudo force acting outward in rotating frame. Magnitude same as centripetal but opposite direction. Used in problems from rotating reference frame.

Fcf = mv²/r (outward)
Numerical Problem 3

A 0.5kg stone is whirled in horizontal circle of radius 1.5m at 2 revolutions per second. Find centripetal force.

f = 2 Hz, ω = 2πf = 4π rad/s ≈ 12.57 rad/s
v = rω = 1.5 × 12.57 = 18.855 m/s
Fc = mv²/r = 0.5 × (18.855)² / 1.5 = 0.5 × 355.5 / 1.5 = 177.75 / 1.5 = 118.5 N
Alternatively Fc = m r ω² = 0.5 × 1.5 × (12.57)² = 0.75 × 158 = 118.5 N
Numerical Problem 4

A car of mass 1000kg takes a turn of radius 50m at 36 km/h. Find centripetal force.

v = 36 km/h = 10 m/s
Fc = mv²/r = 1000 × (10)² / 50 = 1000 × 100 / 50 = 100000/50 = 2000 N
Numerical Problem 5

A wheel of radius 0.4m accelerates from rest to 20 rad/s in 5s. Find angular acceleration and linear velocity after 5s.

α = (ω - ω₀)/t = (20 - 0)/5 = 4 rad/s²
v = rω = 0.4 × 20 = 8 m/s

Work, Power, Energy

Work

Work done = Force × displacement in force direction

W = F·s·cosθ

SI unit: Joule (J) = N·m

Power

Rate of doing work

P = Work / time = F·v (if constant force)

SI unit: Watt (W) = J/s

Energy

Capacity to do work

  • Kinetic Energy: KE = ½mv²
  • Potential Energy: PE = mgh

SI unit: Joule (J)

Numerical Problem 6

A 10kg box is lifted vertically 2m at constant speed. Find work done against gravity.

Force = mg = 10×9.81 = 98.1 N
Work = F·s = 98.1 × 2 = 196.2 J
Numerical Problem 7

A force of 50N moves a body 10m at 30° to displacement. Find work done.

W = F·s·cosθ = 50 × 10 × cos30° = 500 × 0.866 = 433 J
Numerical Problem 8

A motor lifts 500kg at constant speed of 2 m/s. Find power output.

Force = mg = 500×9.81 = 4905 N
Power = F·v = 4905 × 2 = 9810 W = 9.81 kW
Numerical Problem 9

A 0.2kg ball is thrown upward with 15 m/s. Find KE at start and PE at top (g=10 m/s²).

KE = ½mv² = 0.5×0.2×225 = 0.1×225 = 22.5 J
At top, v=0, height = v²/(2g) = 225/(20)=11.25m
PE = mgh = 0.2×10×11.25 = 22.5 J (energy conservation)

Practice: A 1200kg car accelerates from 20 km/h to 50 km/h. Find increase in KE.

v₁ = 20 km/h = 5.556 m/s, v₂ = 50 km/h = 13.889 m/s
KE₁ = ½×1200×(5.556)² = 600×30.86 = 18516 J
KE₂ = ½×1200×(13.889)² = 600×192.9 = 115740 J
ΔKE = 115740 - 18516 = 97224 J = 97.2 kJ
