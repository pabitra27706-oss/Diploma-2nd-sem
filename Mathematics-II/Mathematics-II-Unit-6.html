Measures of Central Tendency: Mean, Median, Mode

Mean (Arithmetic)

Ungrouped: \( \bar{x} = \frac{\sum x_i}{n} \)

Grouped: \( \bar{x} = \frac{\sum f_i x_i}{\sum f_i} \) (where \( x_i \) = class mark)

Median

Ungrouped: Middle value when data sorted. If n odd: \( \frac{n+1}{2} \)th term. If n even: average of \( \frac{n}{2} \)th and \( \frac{n}{2}+1 \)th terms.

Grouped: \( M = L + \frac{\frac{N}{2} - cf}{f} \times h \)

Mode

Ungrouped: Most frequent value.

Grouped: \( Z = L + \frac{f_1 - f_0}{2f_1 - f_0 - f_2} \times h \)

Example: Ungrouped Data

Find mean, median, mode for: 12, 15, 12, 18, 20, 15, 12, 16, 18, 20

Mean: \( \frac{12+15+12+18+20+15+12+16+18+20}{10} = \frac{158}{10} = 15.8 \)
Median: Sorted: 12,12,12,15,15,16,18,18,20,20 → median = avg of 5th and 6th = (15+16)/2 = 15.5
Mode: 12 appears 3 times → mode = 12

Measures of Dispersion: Standard Deviation

Standard Deviation (σ)

Ungrouped: \( \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum (x_i - \bar{x})^2}{n}} \)

Grouped: \( \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum f_i (x_i - \bar{x})^2}{\sum f_i}} \)

Also: \( \sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum f_i x_i^2}{\sum f_i} - \left(\frac{\sum f_i x_i}{\sum f_i}\right)^2} \)

Example: Ungrouped SD

Find SD for: 5, 8, 10, 12, 15

Mean \( \bar{x} = (5+8+10+12+15)/5 = 50/5 = 10 \)
Deviations: -5, -2, 0, 2, 5; squares: 25,4,0,4,25; sum = 58
\( \sigma = \sqrt{58/5} = \sqrt{11.6} \approx 3.41 \)

Probability: Definitions & Classical Approach

Basic Definitions
  • Random experiment: An experiment whose outcome cannot be predicted in advance (e.g., tossing a coin).
  • Sample space (S): Set of all possible outcomes (e.g., {H, T} for a coin).
  • Event: A subset of the sample space (e.g., getting a head).
  • Occurrence of event: Event E occurs if the outcome is in E.

Types of Events

  • Impossible event: \( \emptyset \) (probability 0)
  • Mutually exclusive: Events cannot occur together ( \( A \cap B = \emptyset \) )
  • Exhaustive events: Union covers entire sample space
  • Equally likely events: Each outcome has same chance

Classical Probability

If an experiment has \( n \) equally likely outcomes, and an event E has \( m \) favorable outcomes, then:

\[ P(E) = \frac{m}{n} \]
Problem 1: Coin Toss

Find probability of getting a head when tossing a fair coin.

Sample space S = {H, T}, n = 2, favorable outcomes {H} → m = 1
\( P = \frac{1}{2} \)
Problem 2: Dice Roll

Find probability of getting an even number when rolling a fair die.

S = {1,2,3,4,5,6}, n = 6. Even numbers = {2,4,6} → m = 3
\( P = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2} \)
Problem 3: Cards

From a well-shuffled deck of 52 cards, find probability of drawing a king.

Total outcomes n = 52, kings = 4 → m = 4
\( P = \frac{4}{52} = \frac{1}{13} \)
Problem 4: Mutually Exclusive

If A and B are mutually exclusive events with P(A)=0.3, P(B)=0.4, find P(A∪B).

Since mutually exclusive, \( P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) = 0.3 + 0.4 = 0.7 \)
