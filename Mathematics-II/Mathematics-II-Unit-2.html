Coordinate Systems · Distance · Division · Area

Cartesian & Polar Coordinates

Cartesian: \( (x, y) \). Polar: \( (r, \theta) \) where \( r = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}, \theta = \tan^{-1}(y/x) \).

Relations: \( x = r\cos\theta, y = r\sin\theta \).

Distance Formula

\( d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2} \)

Internal Division

Point dividing \( A(x_1,y_1) \) and \( B(x_2,y_2) \) in ratio \( m:n \) internally:

\( \left( \frac{mx_2 + nx_1}{m+n}, \frac{my_2 + ny_1}{m+n} \right) \)

External Division

\( \left( \frac{mx_2 - nx_1}{m-n}, \frac{my_2 - ny_1}{m-n} \right) \)

Area of Triangle

\( \Delta = \frac{1}{2} \left| x_1(y_2 - y_3) + x_2(y_3 - y_1) + x_3(y_1 - y_2) \right| \)

Collinearity: Area = 0 (points lie on same line).

Problem: Distance & Division

Find the point dividing \( A(2,3) \) and \( B(5,7) \) in ratio 2:3 internally.

\[ x = \frac{2\cdot5 + 3\cdot2}{2+3} = \frac{10+6}{5} = \frac{16}{5} = 3.2 \] \[ y = \frac{2\cdot7 + 3\cdot3}{5} = \frac{14+9}{5} = \frac{23}{5} = 4.6 \] Point: \( (3.2, 4.6) \)

Straight Line: Slope, Equations, Angle, Distance

Slope (Gradient)

\( m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} = \tan\theta \) (θ = inclination).

Slope-Intercept

\( y = mx + c \)

Point-Slope

\( y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \)

Two-Point

\( \frac{y - y_1}{y_2 - y_1} = \frac{x - x_1}{x_2 - x_1} \)

Intercept Form

\( \frac{x}{a} + \frac{y}{b} = 1 \)

Angle Between Two Lines

\( \tan\theta = \left| \frac{m_2 - m_1}{1 + m_1 m_2} \right| \)

Parallel: \( m_1 = m_2 \)

Perpendicular: \( m_1 m_2 = -1 \)

Perpendicular Distance

From point \( (x_1,y_1) \) to line \( ax+by+c=0 \):

\( d = \frac{|ax_1 + by_1 + c|}{\sqrt{a^2 + b^2}} \)

Distance between parallel lines \( ax+by+c_1=0 \) and \( ax+by+c_2=0 \):

\( d = \frac{|c_1 - c_2|}{\sqrt{a^2 + b^2}} \)

Problem: Angle & Perpendicular

Find angle between lines \( y = 2x+3 \) and \( 3x - y + 1 = 0 \).

\( m_1 = 2 \), \( m_2 = 3 \) (from \( y = 3x+1 \))
\[ \tan\theta = \left| \frac{3-2}{1+2\cdot3} \right| = \frac{1}{7} \] \( \theta = \tan^{-1}(1/7) \approx 8.13^\circ \)

Circle: Equations, Centre, Radius

Centre-Radius Form

\( (x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2 \) with centre \( (h,k) \), radius \( r \).

Diameter Form

If \( A(x_1,y_1) \) and \( B(x_2,y_2) \) are ends of diameter, equation:

\( (x - x_1)(x - x_2) + (y - y_1)(y - y_2) = 0 \)

General Equation

\( x^2 + y^2 + 2gx + 2fy + c = 0 \)

Centre: \( (-g, -f) \), Radius \( = \sqrt{g^2 + f^2 - c} \)

Problem: Circle from General Equation

Find centre and radius of \( x^2 + y^2 - 4x + 6y - 12 = 0 \).

\( 2g = -4 \Rightarrow g = -2 \), \( 2f = 6 \Rightarrow f = 3 \)
Centre \( (-g, -f) = (2, -3) \)
Radius \( = \sqrt{(-2)^2 + 3^2 - (-12)} = \sqrt{4+9+12} = \sqrt{25} = 5 \)

Conic Sections: Parabola · Ellipse

Definition of Conic

A conic is the locus of a point whose distance from a fixed point (focus) and fixed line (directrix) are in constant ratio \( e \) (eccentricity).

  • Vertex: point where conic meets axis
  • Axis: line through focus perpendicular to directrix
  • Eccentricity e: for parabola \( e=1 \), ellipse \( e<1 \)
  • Latus rectum: chord through focus perpendicular to axis

Parabola (Standard Equation)

\( y^2 = 4ax \) (opens right)

Vertex: \( (0,0) \), Focus: \( (a,0) \), Directrix: \( x = -a \), Latus rectum length: \( 4a \)

Ellipse (Standard Equation)

\( \frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1 \) (a > b)

Centre: \( (0,0) \), Foci: \( (\pm ae, 0) \), Eccentricity \( e = \sqrt{1 - \frac{b^2}{a^2}} \)

Problem: Parabola

Find focus, directrix of parabola \( y^2 = 12x \).

\( 4a = 12 \Rightarrow a = 3 \)
Focus: \( (3,0) \), Directrix: \( x = -3 \)
Problem: Ellipse

Find eccentricity of ellipse \( \frac{x^2}{25} + \frac{y^2}{9} = 1 \).

\( a^2 = 25 \Rightarrow a = 5 \), \( b^2 = 9 \Rightarrow b = 3 \)
\( e = \sqrt{1 - \frac{9}{25}} = \sqrt{\frac{16}{25}} = \frac{4}{5} = 0.8 \)
