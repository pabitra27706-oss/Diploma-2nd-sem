Definition & Meaning of Partial Derivative

∂/∂x Partial Derivative

For a function \( f(x,y) \) of two variables, the partial derivative with respect to \( x \) at \( (a,b) \) is:

\[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}(a,b) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(a+h, b) - f(a,b)}{h} \]

Similarly, with respect to \( y \):

\[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}(a,b) = \lim_{k \to 0} \frac{f(a, b+k) - f(a,b)}{k} \]

Meaning: It represents the rate of change of \( f \) as one variable changes while the other is held constant.

Evaluation of Partial Derivatives

How to Compute

To find \( \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \), treat \( y \) as constant and differentiate normally with respect to \( x \).

To find \( \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} \), treat \( x \) as constant and differentiate with respect to \( y \).

Example 1

If \( f(x,y) = x^3 y^2 + 2x + y \), find \( \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \) and \( \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} \).

\[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 3x^2 y^2 + 2 \] \[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = 2x^3 y + 1 \]
Example 2

If \( f(x,y) = e^{xy} \sin x \), find \( \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \) and \( \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} \).

\[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = e^{xy}(y \sin x + \cos x) \] \[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = e^{xy} (x \sin x) \]
Example 3: Second Order

For \( f(x,y) = x^2 y + y^2 \), find \( \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2} \) and \( \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y^2} \).

\[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 2xy \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2} = 2y \] \[ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = x^2 + 2y \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y^2} = 2 \]

Homogeneous Functions: Definition & Examples

Definition

A function \( f(x,y) \) is said to be homogeneous of degree \( n \) if for any scalar \( t \),

\[ f(tx, ty) = t^n f(x,y) \]
Example 1

Check homogeneity of \( f(x,y) = x^3 + 2x^2 y + y^3 \).

\[ f(tx, ty) = (tx)^3 + 2(tx)^2(ty) + (ty)^3 = t^3 x^3 + 2 t^3 x^2 y + t^3 y^3 = t^3 (x^3 + 2x^2 y + y^3) \] So homogeneous of degree 3.
Example 2

Is \( f(x,y) = \frac{x^2 + y^2}{x + y} \) homogeneous? Find degree.

\[ f(tx, ty) = \frac{(tx)^2 + (ty)^2}{tx + ty} = \frac{t^2(x^2 + y^2)}{t(x+y)} = t \cdot \frac{x^2 + y^2}{x+y} = t f(x,y) \] Homogeneous of degree 1.

Euler's Theorem on Homogeneous Functions (2 variables)

Euler's Theorem (Statement only)

If \( f(x,y) \) is a homogeneous function of degree \( n \), then:

\[ x \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} + y \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = n f(x,y) \]

This holds for all \( x,y \) where the partial derivatives exist.

Problem 1

Verify Euler's theorem for \( f(x,y) = x^3 + y^3 + 2x^2 y \).

First, check homogeneity: \( f(tx,ty) = t^3(x^3 + y^3 + 2x^2 y) \), so degree \( n=3 \).
Compute partials:
\( \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = 3x^2 + 4xy \)
\( \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} = 3y^2 + 2x^2 \)
LHS = \( x(3x^2 + 4xy) + y(3y^2 + 2x^2) = 3x^3 + 4x^2 y + 3y^3 + 2x^2 y = 3x^3 + 6x^2 y + 3y^3 \)
RHS = \( 3(x^3 + y^3 + 2x^2 y) = 3x^3 + 3y^3 + 6x^2 y \) ✔
Problem 2

If \( f(x,y) = \frac{x^2 + y^2}{x+y} \), use Euler's theorem to find \( x f_x + y f_y \).

From earlier, \( f \) is homogeneous of degree 1.
By Euler's theorem: \( x f_x + y f_y = 1 \cdot f = \frac{x^2 + y^2}{x+y} \)
Problem 3

Given \( u = \tan^{-1}\left( \frac{x^3 + y^3}{x - y} \right) \), show that \( x \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + y \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} = \sin 2u \).

Let \( v = \frac{x^3 + y^3}{x - y} \). Then \( u = \tan^{-1} v \).
Check homogeneity of \( v \): \( v(tx,ty) = t^2 \cdot \frac{x^3 + y^3}{x - y} = t^2 v \) ⇒ degree 2.
By Euler: \( x v_x + y v_y = 2v \).
Now \( \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} = \frac{1}{1+v^2} \frac{\partial v}{\partial x} \), similarly for y.
Then \( x u_x + y u_y = \frac{1}{1+v^2} (x v_x + y v_y) = \frac{2v}{1+v^2} \).
But \( v = \tan u \), so \( \frac{2\tan u}{1+\tan^2 u} = 2 \cdot \frac{\tan u}{\sec^2 u} = 2 \sin u \cos u = \sin 2u \). ✔
